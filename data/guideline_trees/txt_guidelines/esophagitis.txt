Assess severity of Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)(esophagitis) symptoms:
Mild and intermittent symptoms (fewer than two episodesper week) and no evidence of erosive esophagitis;
Erosive esophagitis, Barrett's esophagus, frequent symptoms (two or more episodes per week), and/or severe symptomsthat impair quality of life.

If Mild and intermittent GERD symptoms:
Action:
Initial management with the following:
1. Initiate and continue lifestyle and dietary modification.
2. Low dose H2RA prn.
3. Antacids with or without sodiumalginate if symptoms <1 per week.
4. Assess symptomatic response in 4 Weeks.

if Severe or frequent GERD symptoms, erosive esophagitis or Barrett's esophagus:
Action:
1. Lifestyle and dietary modification.
2. Standard dose PPI once daily.

if Persistent symptoms:
Action:
1. Reinforce compliance and lifestyle modification.
2. Manage as refractory GERD (Refer toUpToDate topic on refractory GERD formanagement)
