The image contains a flowchart for the management of GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease) symptoms. Here is the text extracted from the flowchart:

Assess severity of GERD symptoms:
- Mild and intermittent symptoms (fewer than two episodes per week) and no evidence of erosive esophagitis
- Erosive esophagitis, Barrett's esophagus, frequent symptoms (two or more episodes per week), and/or severe symptoms that impair quality of life

Mild and intermittent GERD symptoms
- Initial management with the following:
  - Initiate and continue lifestyle and dietary modification
  - Low dose H2RA prn
  - Antacids with or without sodium alginate if symptoms <1 per week
  - Assess symptomatic response in 4 weeks

Persistent symptoms
- Increase H2RA to standard dose, twice daily
- Assess symptomatic response in 2 weeks

Symptom improvement/resolution
- Continue lifestyle and dietary modification
- H2RA prn

Severe or frequent GERD symptoms, erosive esophagitis or Barrett's esophagus
- Lifestyle and dietary modification
- Standard dose PPI once daily

Persistent symptoms
- Reinforce compliance and lifestyle modification
- Manage as refractory GERD (Refer to UpToDate topic on refractory GERD for management)

Persistent symptoms
- Discontinue H2RA
- Initiate low dose PPI once daily
- Assess symptomatic response in 4 to 8 weeks
